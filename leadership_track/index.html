Leadership Career Track

People leaders focus on the health and performance of a team and the individuals within the team. The output of the team is their responsibility. A healthy team has engaged, empowered, and motivated employees. People leaders encourage development, have honest conversations about performance, and have a dedication to quality work. We look to people leaders to have a long-term perspective and help manage through change within the organization. They are relationship builders within their team and beyond. Leaders are more visible stewards of company culture, values and attitudes. This includes keeping the good of the customer front of mind. Leaders are dedicated to honesty, integrity, and creating an environment of inclusion and value. Their level of transparency and clear communication inspire trust and understanding. Some of the skills for leaders remain the same at each level of leadership, but a leader should become more and more proficient with these as they progress along the management track.


Leadership Roles

There are different roles and levels within the leadership track:

  • Engineering Manager
  • Engineering Department Lead
  • Director of Engineering Department Leadership
  • Engineering Program Manager
  • Director of Engineering Program Management
  • Director of Architecture


Attribute Categories

There are many ways to categorize the attributes that make a people leader successful, but these four categories are particular to our organization's software development culture. These categories represent the key areas of development and mastery for Engineering Leadership roles.

Technical Skill [T]

Have you been seeking to improve your craft? Are you retaining the level of technical competence necessary to understand and direct the work of your team and to communicate technical issues and resolutions between your team and program leadership?


Productivity [P]

How does your work align with OSIsoft’s overarching goals? Does your team or program consistently deliver complete features? Are your team and program processes and backlogs aligned with department guidelines to facilitate collaboration within programs and accurately track progress? Do you follow up on administrative tasks in a timely fashion and do you hold your team members accountable for doing the same? Are you contributing to department efforts in staffing, recruiting and hiring to find more talented people who can help us accomplish our goals?


Empathy [E]

How receptive are you to outside feedback on the work of your team or program? Do you foster a spirit of collaboration within your team and with other teams in a program? Are you working with others to achieve the goals of the program? Do members of your team (or program) step in to help other teams that can benefit from their experience, skillset or time?


Influence [I]

How effectively are you communicating team and program goals? Do you help our teams stay focused on the right priorities and remove impediments quickly? Are your team members generally engaged and motivated to help the company reach our goals? Do you dedicate considerable time to the career development of individual members of your team and encourage others on your team to assist in mentoring new employees and interns?


Skills all people leader roles exercise:

The skills expressed in people leadership are similar across roles, so we will list them once with the understanding that they are exercised with more proficiency at higher levels.

  • Inspire and motivate others.
  • Exhibit high integrity and honesty.
  • Respect team members and value their perspectives.
  • Solve problems and analyze issues.
  • Communicate clearly and well.
  • Conduct regular 1-on-1 meetings with team members.
  • Actively listen with empathy.
  • Collaborate and promote teamwork.
  • Handle conflict well.
  • Display technical and professional expertise.
  • Develop others.
  • Take initiative.


Role Descriptions

Each description has two parts: a high-level description, and common attributes. Attributes are tagged by the categories described above: (T) Technical Skill, (P) Productivity, (E) Empathy, and (I) Influence. These attributes aren’t an exhaustive checklist but rather a guideline for behavior we would expect from someone in that role.

Engineering Manager

The Engineering Manager is responsible for a single team with a common goal. They focus on growing the skills of their team. They are role models and they instill an attitude and perspective that is aligned with OSIsoft's core values. They are the primary resource for questions regarding process as well as work content. They are focused mostly on daily and weekly execution through standard processes. They help the team focus and execute against their goals. They keep the energy and standards high. Their decisions impact their immediate team and work product. While the focus of an Engineering Manager is on people and process, they must also maintain their technical competence in order to understand, explain and resolve impediments related to the technical work of their teams.

Attributes

  • Display technical competence in software development and comprehensive understanding of OSIsoft products. [T]
  • Actively participate in program meetings. [T, P]
  • Delegate the appropriate technical discussions and decisions to their team. [T, I]
  • In coordination with technical leadership, set high standards and ensure the quality of their team's work and adherence to best practices (e.g. automated testing). [T]
  • Direct team members to known technical experts outside of the team and encourage them to build a strong network. [P]
  • Collaborate with Product Management to ensure the team backlog is prioritized appropriately. [P, E]
  • Share ownership of program goals and encourage team members to assist other teams in completing them. [P, E]
  • Welcome help from other teams, encouraging their own team members to share expertise on products, technologies and codebases. [P, E]
  • Understand and practice standard processes and maintain a team backlog according to program standards [P, E]
  • Estimate features to program milestones. [P]
  • Support program management goals through sprint planning and communication to their team. [P]
  • Publish sprint reports showing work committed, work completed, explanations for disparities and plans to address them. [P]
  • Have productivity measurements, report on them regularly and use them to improve team efficiency. [P]
  • Commit to hard dates and make forecasted dates. [P]
  • Help with recruiting efforts for their team and the department as a whole and encourage members of their team to do the same (events, interviews, etc.). [P]
  • Assess team capabilities and create an appropriate staffing plan. [P]
  • Align with Engineering organization and culture, supporting and respecting the specialization of roles. [E]
  • Keep a team onboarding plan and ensure new team members complete it along with standard company and department onboarding. [I, E]
  • Focus on coaching, empowering, and developing others and giving actionable feedback. [I]
  • Use SMART goals and career development plans to facilitate career growth for all employees and to provide feedback and coaching to underperforming team members. [I, P]
  • Communicate relevant information and foster support for program and department leadership goals with their team. [I]
  • Schedule daily standups, estimation meetings, sprint planning and retrospective meetings. [I]
  • Regularly remove impediments to ensure planned work can be completed. [I]
  • Ensure team members understand their role and appreciate the needs of their team. [I]
  • Handle administrative responsibilities (e.g., expenses and time-reporting approvals, keeping calendar current, ensuring employee profiles are complete in HR system, etc.) and complete related tasks in a timely manner.[E]

Ideally an Engineering Manager has experience as a Senior Software Developer. Future career path could include Engineering Program Manager or Engineering Department Lead. An Engineering Manager reports to an Engineering Department Lead.

Engineering Department Lead

The Engineering Department Lead is responsible for a group of Engineering Managers and Staff Software Developers, and in some cases Guild Managers and Principal Software Developers. Engineering Department Leads typically represent one office or region but may represent multiple locations. They are familiar and proficient with the function of an Engineering Manager. They are responsible for the skill growth of the team and Engineering Managers in particular. The primary goal of the Engineering Department Lead is to help their managers build high-performing teams. They defer to Architecture to help with the skill growth of Principal and Staff Software Developers. They participate with other Engineering Department Leads to set people and performance vision and standards. They also translate that vision and standards to their teams. They are responsible for the output and growth of the team at a large scale. They are responsible for maintaining the collective skills and capacity of the software development teams and recruiting for the needs of the future. Specific duties include:

Attributes

  • Conduct one on one conversations with their team members. [I, E]
  • Coach Engineering Managers in people development skills. [I, P]
  • Enable productivity and high standards for the larger team. [I, P]
  • Involved with more strategic initiatives to support the team. [I, P, E]
  • Assessing staffing needs across teams. [P]
  • Anticipate and provide for regular / typical onboarding needs of new members. [I]
  • Coordinate department recruiting and staffing. [P]
  • Promote communication and alignment throughout the department. [I, E, P]

Ideally the Engineering Department Lead has experience as a Senior Software Developer and Engineering Manager. The Engineering Department Lead reports to the Director of Engineering Department Leadership.

Director of Engineering Department Leadership

The main objective of the Director of Engineering Department Leadership is to provide consistency in approach across people and process management while providing career guidance and facilitating the growth of the Engineering Department Leads. Specific duties of the Director of Engineering Department Leadership may include:

Attributes

  • Shape the direction of Engineering Department Leadership. [I]
  • Provide mentoring and career planning advice to the Engineering Department Leads. [I]
  • Ensure consistency across management of people and processes within Engineering. [P]
  • Schedule and conduct coordination meetings between Engineering Department Leads. [I, E]
  • Report on engineering people and process management to the Vice President of Engineering. [P, I]
  • Identify, recruit, and onboard new Engineering Department Leads. [P, I]

Ideally the Director of Engineering Department Leadership has experience as a Senior Software Developer, Engineering Manager, and Engineering Department Lead. The Director of Engineering Department Leadership reports to the Vice President of Engineering. 

Engineering Program Manager

The Engineering Program Manager’s main objective is to collaborate with product management and architecture to drive a specific program to completion by coordinating that program’s needs across the program’s engineering teams. While the product management team represents the customer needs and requirements (the “why”), and the architecture team provides the technical guidance (the “what”), the engineering program management team focuses on the processes and procedures across the engaged engineering teams (the “how”). Specific duties of an Engineering Program Manager may include:

Attributes

  • Identify the skills required of the engineering teams necessary to execute the program. [P, T]
  • Understand and communicate to engineering the goals of the program, especially to the teams involved in the program. [P, I]
  • Schedule and conduct sprint reviews for the program. [P, I]
  • Establish procedures for collecting and organizing the program’s artifacts (i.e. meeting recordings). [P, I]
  • Ensure consistency of procedures across the teams engaged in the program (i.e. consistent use of work items). [P] 
  • Coordinate with other programs (i.e. prioritize disruptions to the program). [P, E]
  • Manage any program pre-releases (i.e. Private Preview). [P, I]
  • Collaborate with Customer Service to ensure proper support for the program. [E]
  • Represent the program at and coordinate its contribution to quarterly reviews. [I]
  • Report on the program to the Director of Engineering Program Management. [P]
  • Schedule and conduct coordination meetings between team leads for the teams involved in the program. [P, E, I]
  • Coordinate the program’s representation at regional seminars and users conferences (PI World). [P]

Ideally the Engineering Program Manager has experience as a Senior Software Developer and Engineering Manager. Future career path could include Director of Engineering Program Management, Engineering Department Lead, Director of Engineering Department Leadership, and Vice President of Engineering. The Engineering Program Manager reports to the Director of Engineering Program Management.

Director of Engineering Program Management

The main objective of the Director of Engineering Program Management is to provide consistency in approach across engineering programs while providing career guidance and facilitating the growth of the Engineering Program Managers. Specific duties of the Director of Engineering Program Management may include:

Attributes

  • Shape the direction of Engineering Program Management. [I]
  • Provide mentoring and career planning advice to the Engineering Program Managers. [I]
  • Ensure consistency across programs (i.e. documentation of standard procedures and processes). [P] 
  • Schedule and conduct coordination meetings between program managers and programs. [P, E, I]
  • Report on engineering program management to the Vice President of Engineering. [P, I]
  • Identify, recruit, and onboard new Engineering Program Managers. [P, I]
  • Coordinate with the Directors of Architecture and Department Leadership. [P, E, I]

Ideally the Director of Engineering Program Management has experience as a Senior Software Developer, Engineering Manager, and Engineering Program Manager. The Director of Engineering Program Management reports to the Vice President of Engineering.

Director of Architecture

The main objective of the Director of Architecture is to provide consistency in approach across OSIsoft’s technology platforms. Specific duties of the Director of Architecture include:

Attributes

  • Provide strategic leadership and oversight to all aspects of system architecture and OSIsoft’s technology direction. [T, I]
  • Provide leadership and direction for technical architecture and decision making across teams of Software Architects, Principal Software Engineers and Software Developers in a direct or matrixed management environment. [T, P, I]
  • Lead and participate in the planning, architecture, and design of all software within OSIsoft. [T, P, I]
  • Drive and ensure technical collaboration across teams and engineering programs from conception to aftermarket maintenance activities. [P, E]
  • Ensure technical architectures are aligned with technical, business, and platform architecture objectives and directions. [T, P]
  • Develop and implement strategies for architectural standards, software development best practices, and system level performance metrics. [T]
  • Lead all aspects of the technical architecture and design of software, web, cloud, and mobile applications, without a bias toward specific technologies. [T]
  • Accept new ideas and establish a culture of open and objective review. [E]
  • Provide mentorship to the Software Development teams in identifying common architectural patterns and requirements. [T]
  • Serve as primary liaison between Software Development and the Architecture team. [I, P]
  • Ensure all architects and software developers keep current with architectural and technological developments. [T, I]
  • Drive teamwork and partnership across the Engineering “Triad” of Product, Software Development, and Architecture. [E, I]
  • Lead research and development for system architecture within the main Engineering department of OSIsoft and provide leadership for innovation within and across software development teams. [T, P, I]

Ideally the Director of Architecture has experience as a Software Architect and as an experienced technical manager leading a high-performance team. The Director of Architecture reports to the Vice President of Software Development.
