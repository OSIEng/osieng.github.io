Leadership Career Track

Introduction

People leaders focus on the health and performance of a team and the individuals within the team. The output of the team is their responsibility. A healthy team has engaged, empowered, and motivated employees. People leaders encourage development, have honest conversations about performance, and have a dedication to quality work. We look to people leaders to have a long-term perspective and help manage through change within the organization. They are relationship builders within their team and beyond. Leaders are more visible stewards of company culture, values and attitudes. This includes keeping the good of the customer front of mind. Leaders are dedicated to honesty, integrity, and creating an environment of inclusion and value. Their level of transparency and clear communication inspire trust and understanding. The skills for leaders remain the same at each level of leadership, but they should become more and more proficient with them as they progress along the management track.


Leadership Roles

There are different roles and levels within the guild track:

  • Engineering Manager
  • Engineering Program Manager
  • Director of Engineering Program Management
  • Engineering Department Lead
  • Director of Engineering Department Leadership


Level Descriptions

Each description has three parts: a high-level description, specific things that they do in the role, and skills that people express. The skills expressed in people leadership are similar across levels, so we will list them once with the understanding that they are exercised with more proficiency at higher levels.


Engineering Manager

The Engineering Manager is responsible for a single team with a common goal. They have a high level of focus on the skill growth of their team and modeling and instilling an attitude and perspective that is aligned with OSIsoft. They are the primary resource for process questions and can answer questions about work content as well. They are focused mostly on daily and weekly execution through standard processes. They help the team focus and execute against their goals. They keep the energy and standards high. Their decisions impact their immediate team and work product.

Specific things they do

  • Actively listens with empathy
  • Works with employees to coordinate work activities and resolve issues
  • Conducts one on one conversations with their team members
  • Sets team performance and skill growth goals
  • Involved with day to day tasks of team
  • Involved in assessing staffing needs and appropriate levels
  • Participates in recruiting and engages team member help
  • Establishes strong connection with new employees early

Skills they exercise (for all people lead roles)

  • Inspiring and Motivating Others
  • Exhibits high integrity and honesty
  • Solves problems, and analyzes issues
  • Communicates clearly and well
  • Collaborates and promotes teamwork
  • Displays technical and professional expertise
  • Develops others
  • Takes initiative

Engineering Program Manager

The Engineering Program Manager’s main objective is to collaborate with product management and architecture to drive a specific program to completion by coordinating that program’s needs across the program’s engineering teams. While the product management team represents the customer needs and requirements (the “why”), and the architecture team provides the technical guidance (the “what”), the engineering program management team focuses on the processes and procedures across the engaged engineering teams (the “how”). Specific duties of an Engineering Program Manager may include:

  • Identify the skills required of the engineering teams necessary to execute the program. 
  • Understand and communicate to engineering the goals of the program, especially to the teams involved in the program. 
  • Schedule and conduct sprint reviews for the program. 
  • Establish procedures for collecting and organizing the program’s artifacts (i.e. meeting recordings). 
  • Ensure consistency of procedures across the teams engaged in the program (i.e. consistent use of work items). 
  • Coordinate with other programs (i.e. prioritize disruptions to the program).
  • Manage any program pre-releases (i.e. Private Preview). 
  • Collaborate with Customer Service to ensure proper support for the program. 
  • Represent the program at and coordinate its contribution to quarterly reviews. 
  • Report on the program to the Director of Engineering Program Management. 
  • Schedule and conduct coordination meetings between Engineering Managers for the teams involved in the program. 
  • Coordinate the program’s representation at regional seminars and users conferences (PI World).

Ideally the Engineering Program Manager has experience as a Senior Software Developer and Engineering Manager. Future career path could include Director of Engineering Program Management, Engineering Department Lead, Director of Engineering Department Leadership, and Vice President of Engineering. The Engineering Program Manager reports to the Director of Engineering Program Management.

Director of Engineering Program Management

The main objective of the Director of Engineering Program Management is to provide consistency in approach across engineering programs while providing career guidance and facilitating the growth of the Engineering Program Managers. Specific duties of the Director of Engineering Program Management may include:

  • Shape the direction of Engineering Program Management. 
  • Provide mentoring and career planning advice to the Engineering Program Managers. 
  • Ensure consistency across programs (i.e. documentation of standard procedures and processes). 
  • Schedule and conduct coordination meetings between program managers and programs. 
  • Report on engineering program management to the Vice President of Engineering. 
  • Identify, recruit, and onboard new Engineering Program Managers. 

Ideally the Director of Engineering Program Management has experience as a Senior Software Developer, Engineering Manager, and Engineering Program Manager. The Director of Engineering Program Management reports to the Vice President of Engineering.

Engineering Department Lead

The Engineering Department Lead is responsible for a group of Engineering Managers. They typically represent one office or region but may represent multiple locations. They are familiar and proficient with the function of an Engineering Manager. They are responsible for the skill growth of the team and Engineering Managers. They participate with other Engineering Department Leads to set people and performance vision and standards. They also translate that vision and standards to their teams. They are responsible for the output and growth of the team at a large scale and interact with Engineering Managers and their individual teams. They are responsible for maintaining the collective skills and capacity of the team and recruiting for the needs of the future. Specific duties include:

  • Actively listen with empathy
  • Conduct one on one conversations with their team members
  • Coach Engineering Managers in people development skills
  • Enable productivity and high standards for the larger team
  • Involved with more strategic initiatives to support the team
  • Assessing staffing needs across teams
  • Anticipates and provides for regular / typical onboarding needs of new members

Ideally the Engineering Department Lead has experience as a Senior Software Developer and Engineering Manager. The Engineering Department Lead reports to the Director of Engineering Department Leadership.

Director of Engineering Department Leadership

The main objective of the Director of Engineering Department Leadership is to provide consistency in approach across people and process management while providing career guidance and facilitating the growth of the Engineering Department Leads. Specific duties of the Director of Engineering Department Leadership may include:

  • Shape the direction of Engineering Department Leadership.
  • Provide mentoring and career planning advice to the Engineering Department Leads.
  • Ensure consistency across management of people and processes within Engineering.
  • Schedule and conduct coordination meetings between Engineering Department Leads.
  • Report on engineering people and process management to the Vice President of Engineering.
  • Identify, recruit, and onboard new Engineering Department Leads.

Ideally the Director of Engineering Department Leadership has experience as a Senior Software Developer, Engineering Manager, and Engineering Department Lead. The Director of Engineering Department Leadership reports to the Vice President of Engineering.
